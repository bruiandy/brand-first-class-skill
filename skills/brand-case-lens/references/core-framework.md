# Core Framework

## Core Belief

This framework looks at brand growth by returning to the base first. When old traffic bonuses fade, the right move is often not to search for the next trick, but to re-check the brand's own resources, operating reality, brand/product clarity, and loop logic.

## The Three-Stage Path

### 1. First Stand Firm

- What resources actually matter here
- What unfair advantage may exist
- Whether the business reality supports the move

### 2. Then Say It Clearly

- Whether the brand has a clear value expression
- Whether the product solves a real user task
- Whether narrative and experience support each other

### 3. Then Run It Through

- Whether there is a minimum repeatable path to acquisition and conversion
- Whether the move is channel-fit, not just attention-seeking
- Whether the team is validating one loop or spreading itself too thin

## The Five Lenses

### Resource Lens

What real long board makes this move possible

### Operating Lens

What category, margin, channel, or organization constraints shape the move

### Brand Lens

What the case says about identity, positioning, and expression

### Product Lens

What user task, scene, and value transition the product is really serving

### Closed-Loop Lens

What path likely connected content, distribution, conversion, and repeatability

## Anti-Rigid Principles

- Use the framework to improve judgment, not to flatten every case into the same answer.
- Choose the most relevant lens first.
- State uncertainty, missing evidence, and plausible alternatives.
- If the move is mostly distribution logic or founder resource logic, say that directly.
